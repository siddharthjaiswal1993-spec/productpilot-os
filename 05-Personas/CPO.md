# Persona: Chief Product Officer

## Overview

**Name:** David Park
**Title:** Chief Product Officer
**Company:** 1,200-person enterprise SaaS (public), workflow automation platform
**Team:** 3 VPs of Product, 8 Group PMs, 35+ PMs total, reporting to CEO
**Experience:** 15 years in product, 2 years as CPO

David is accountable to the CEO and board for product strategy, product-market fit at enterprise scale, and the quality of the product organization's decision-making. He manages 3 VP Products, each owning a distinct product line, and is responsible for cross-product portfolio intelligence and strategic coherence.

---

## Goals

- Build an accountable product organization where decisions are traceable, evidence-backed, and strategically aligned at every level
- Provide the board with quantitative evidence of product organization quality — not just feature ship velocity
- Reduce decision-making time and increase decision quality simultaneously across 35+ PMs
- Ensure cross-product strategic coherence — investments in Platform, Core, and Ecosystem are complementary
- Create an organizational knowledge system that survives PM turnover

---

## Pain Points

**Pain 1: No portfolio-level decision quality visibility**
"I have 35 PMs making dozens of roadmap decisions every week. I have no way of knowing what percentage of those decisions are backed by real evidence versus assembled from memory and stakeholder pressure. I get visibility only when something goes wrong — a customer churns, a sales deal is lost, an engineering team builds the wrong thing."

**Pain 2: Cross-product signal isolation**
"My three product lines share a customer base but have no intelligence sharing. When Enterprise customers escalate to the Platform team, the Core team doesn't hear it. When the Ecosystem team hears a competitive signal, the Core team doesn't know. Cross-product intelligence should be a strategic advantage; today it's an organizational gap."

**Pain 3: Board reporting on product quality**
"I can report ship velocity (features shipped, sprint completion), team metrics (PM headcount, team NPS), and financial metrics (ARR growth, churn). I cannot report product decision quality. There is no metric in my current system that answers 'is the product organization making better or worse decisions than last quarter?'"

**Pain 4: Knowledge loss from PM turnover**
"We lose 5–8 PMs per year to promotion, departure, and restructuring. Each departure takes institutional knowledge with it. By the time a new PM is fully productive in their area, 4–6 months have passed and the previous PM's context is partially reconstructed at best."

---

## Jobs to Be Done

- **When** presenting to the board: provide a quantitative summary of product organization decision quality, strategic alignment, and portfolio health
- **When** conducting PM performance reviews: assess decision quality as a measurable competency with evidence scores and acceptance rates
- **When** a cross-product strategic decision is needed: retrieve cross-product signal synthesis and portfolio intelligence to inform the decision
- **When** onboarding a new VP Product or Group PM: provide access to organizational knowledge graph representing years of decision history

---

## How ProductPilot OS Helps

**CPO Intelligence Dashboard:** A real-time view of evidence-backing rates, confidence scores, and strategic alignment across all 35 PMs and 3 product lines. David can drill into any product line, team, or individual PM's decision quality.

**Board reporting package:** Executive Summary Agent generates a board-ready product intelligence brief with portfolio health score, cross-product strategic coherence analysis, and decision quality trajectory — quarterly and on-demand.

**Cross-product signal intelligence:** Signals from all three product lines flow into a shared intelligence graph. Cross-product patterns (the same customer need appearing across 2 product lines) are surfaced to David and the relevant VPs.

**PM performance metrics:** Evidence quality score, PM acceptance rate, and decision confidence are available per PM — making decision quality a measurable dimension of performance management.

**Knowledge transfer infrastructure:** When a PM leaves, their knowledge graph remains. New PMs onboard with access to the full decision history of their area.

---

## Success Criteria

- Org-wide evidence-backing rate: >75% within 12 months
- Board product quality report: quantitative metrics, not just narrative, in every board package
- Cross-product signal routing: >80% of cross-product signals surface to relevant PMs within 24 hours
- PM onboarding time: <6 weeks to full productivity (down from 3–6 months)
