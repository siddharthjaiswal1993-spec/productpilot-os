# Persona: Customer Success Lead

## Overview

**Name:** Aisha Okonkwo
**Title:** Head of Customer Success
**Company:** 250-person B2B SaaS (Series B), project collaboration platform
**Team:** 8 CSMs, 150 accounts, $22M in managed ARR
**Experience:** 7 years in CS, 2 years at current company

Aisha manages a team of 8 CSMs who collectively cover 150 accounts. She is sitting on an enormous volume of product signal — every week, her team hears 30+ product issues, feature requests, and satisfaction signals from customers. These signals have no systematic path to the product roadmap. They are sent as emails, Slack messages, and CRM notes that get routed to the PM's already-overloaded inbox and frequently lost.

---

## Goals

- Ensure customer signals from her team reliably reach and influence the product roadmap
- Reduce the time her CSMs spend on escalation communication (writing the same context repeatedly to different PMs)
- Provide customers with reliable timelines on feature requests and escalations
- Reduce churn in the accounts her team manages by ensuring product gaps are addressed with evidence and prioritization context
- Build a CS function that is a strategic input to product decisions, not an afterthought

---

## Pain Points

**Pain 1: Signals disappear after she sends them**
"My team sends 20+ product signal emails per week to product managers. We track which signals we send in a spreadsheet. We almost never hear back about what happened to them. Customers ask 'is that feature on the roadmap?' and we don't know. It's embarrassing and it erodes customer trust."

**Pain 2: No business context for escalations**
"When I escalate a product issue, I send a Slack message or email that says 'Customer X is frustrated about Y.' What the PM needs to prioritize it is: ARR at risk, renewal date, competitive alternative this customer is evaluating, and the pattern of similar signals from other accounts. I don't have time to assemble all of that. And without it, my escalation is deprioritized."

**Pain 3: Duplicate escalation effort**
"Multiple CSMs escalate the same product issue from different customers, each writing a separate message to the product team. Nobody is cross-referencing. The product team sees 6 separate messages about the same issue and doesn't know it's a pattern. My team spends 3–4 hours per week writing escalations that would take 30 minutes if there were a system."

**Pain 4: No feedback loop from product**
"When a feature we escalated ships, we rarely get proactive notification. My CSMs find out from customers who discover it themselves. We can't close the loop with customers, which undermines the relationship we're supposed to be building."

---

## Jobs to Be Done

- **When** escalating a product issue: submit it with automatic business context (ARR at risk, renewal date, account tier) rather than assembling that context manually
- **When** a pattern of customer signals is detected: receive a brief showing the pattern and its aggregate business impact, ready to share with the product team
- **When** a product decision is made on an escalation: receive automatic notification with context on the decision, timeline, and what to communicate to the customer
- **When** a customer asks about a feature request status: retrieve the current status and associated evidence from the product knowledge graph

---

## How ProductPilot OS Helps

**CS signal routing:** Aisha's team submits signals through a CS portal. Each signal is automatically enriched with account data (ARR, renewal date, tier, competitive risk) from the CRM integration and routed to the appropriate PM with a prioritized brief.

**Pattern detection across accounts:** When 3+ accounts report the same issue within a 30-day window, the Insight Synthesizer Agent surfaces a pattern brief to both the CS team and the PM — with aggregate business impact and recommended escalation path.

**Closed-loop notification:** When a PM makes a decision on a CS-escalated signal (prioritized, deprioritized, or deferred), the CS team receives an automatic notification with context — what was decided, when it is expected to ship, and what to tell the customer.

**Feature request status:** CS can query the knowledge graph for any customer's escalated requests and see their current status, prioritization score, and expected timeline.

---

## Success Criteria

- CS signal routing time: <10 minutes per escalation (down from 30–45 minutes)
- Signal visibility to product: 100% of CS-submitted signals visible in ProductPilot OS (vs. unknown today)
- Pattern detection: top 3 CS patterns surface to PM weekly
- Customer response time on feature request status: <24 hours (down from days to weeks)
