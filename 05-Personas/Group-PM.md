# Persona: Group Product Manager

## Overview

**Name:** Marcus Thompson
**Title:** Group PM / Head of Product
**Company:** 350-person B2B SaaS (Series C), marketing analytics platform
**Team:** 4 direct-report PMs, 2 designers, 20 engineers
**Experience:** 9 years in product, 2 years at current company

Marcus manages a PM team of 4, each owning a distinct product area: analytics core, integrations, reporting, and mobile. He is responsible for team-level prioritization alignment, PM development, and upward reporting to the VP Product. His most significant challenge is knowing what each PM knows, what signals each PM is seeing, and where the team's collective evidence coverage has gaps.

---

## Goals

- Build a PM team that makes consistently evidence-backed decisions without requiring Marcus's hands-on involvement in every prioritization debate
- Identify where each PM's evidence coverage is thin before it becomes a bad decision
- Provide the VP Product with accurate, consistent reporting on team decision quality and roadmap health
- Develop his PMs toward Senior PM level — which requires demonstrating evidence-backed decision-making consistently
- Reduce the overhead of cross-PM alignment from a weekly meeting to a shared intelligence layer

---

## Pain Points

**Pain 1: Invisible team intelligence coverage gaps**
"I have 4 PMs each synthesizing signals in their own heads. I have no way of knowing which PM has strong evidence for their priorities and which is working from a thin evidence base. I find out when the decision is challenged in a quarterly planning meeting, which is too late."

**Pain 2: Inconsistent PM quality**
"My 4 PMs have very different evidence quality in their decisions. My most experienced PM assembles strong briefs. My newest PM writes PRDs from memory with no citations. I review every PRD, but I can't tell from a quick scan how much of the document is grounded in real customer signals."

**Pain 3: Cross-PM signal duplication and gaps**
"Two of my PMs are hearing the same customer signal — customers requesting better data export capabilities — but neither knows the other is hearing it. They're both half-solving the problem independently. I only discover this in a Monday standup when it comes up in both updates."

**Pain 4: Upward reporting without visibility**
"Every month I present roadmap health to the VP Product. I have to manually collect status from 4 PMs, synthesize it, and format it for an executive audience. This takes a full day and is always at least partially out of date by the time I present it."

---

## Current Workflow

**Weekly:** 4 individual PM 1:1s, team standup, VP Product alignment meeting
**Monthly:** Roadmap health report preparation (full day), 4 PRD reviews, prioritization alignment session
**Quarterly:** Full roadmap review, quarterly planning facilitation, team performance reviews

---

## Jobs to Be Done

- **When** managing team decision quality: see evidence quality scores across all 4 PMs before issues surface in reviews
- **When** identifying cross-PM signal overlap: surface signals that multiple PMs are hearing from different customers to identify platform-level priorities
- **When** coaching a PM: show them specifically where their evidence coverage is thin vs. where it is strong
- **When** reporting upward: generate a team-level intelligence report from the shared evidence base, not from manual collection

---

## How ProductPilot OS Helps

**Team intelligence dashboard:** Marcus sees evidence quality scores, PM acceptance rates, and evidence-backing rates for all 4 PMs on one dashboard. He can identify coaching opportunities before they become decision quality problems.

**Cross-PM signal synthesis:** The Insight Synthesizer Agent surfaces signals that are appearing across multiple PM inboxes — flagging cross-PM patterns that should become team-level priorities.

**PM coaching with evidence:** When Marcus reviews a PM's work, he can see specifically which signals were used, which were available but not used, and where the evidence coverage is thin. Coaching becomes specific and evidence-based.

**Executive summary generation:** The Executive Summary Agent generates a team-level intelligence report from the shared evidence base. Marcus reviews and approves in 30 minutes instead of assembling from 4 separate documents in a full day.

---

## Success Criteria

- Team average evidence-backing rate: >75% within 90 days
- Cross-PM signal coverage gaps identified proactively: >80% before priority debates
- VP Product reporting time: <1 hour/month (down from full day)
- PM quality improvement: 3 of 4 PMs at Senior PM evidence quality standard within 12 months
