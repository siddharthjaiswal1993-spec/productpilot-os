# Persona: Product Manager (IC)

## Overview

**Name:** Alex Chen
**Title:** Product Manager
**Company:** 200-person B2B SaaS (Series B), project management software
**Team:** 4 PMs, 2 designers, 12 engineers
**Experience:** 4 years in product, 2 years at current company

Alex owns the integrations and API product area. Three active features in development, 40+ items in the backlog, and a customer base that generates more signals per week than can be systematically synthesized.

---

## Goals

- Build the right features for the right customers at the right time — not for the loudest voice
- Write PRDs that engineering trusts and stakeholders understand
- Spend more time on strategy and customer discovery, less time on documentation and signal assembly
- Advance to Senior PM within 18 months (which requires demonstrating evidence-backed decision quality)
- Build a reputation for shipping things that customers actually use

---

## Pain Points

**Pain 1: Signal overload without synthesis**
"I start every week with 200 unread Slack messages, 30 new Jira tickets, 8 new Zendesk escalations, and 2 Gong calls I haven't listened to. By the time I've processed 30% of them, it's Wednesday and new signals are already stacking up. I know I'm missing things — I just don't know which things."

**Pain 2: PRDs without customer evidence**
"I write my PRDs from memory. I know what customers need because I've been in the calls, but I can't cite the specific calls or tickets in the document. So when engineering asks 'how many customers need this exact workflow?' I give a range — I don't actually know with precision."

**Pain 3: Prioritization that doesn't survive scrutiny**
"I score everything in RICE, but when I present my priorities, someone always challenges them with a single customer example that contradicts my score. And I can't always defend against it with data because I don't have the assembled data at my fingertips."

**Pain 4: Stakeholder updates eating my Fridays**
"Every Friday I write 3–4 different versions of the same update for different stakeholders. Engineering gets the technical version. My manager gets the strategic version. The customer success team gets the customer impact version. It's the same information, different framing, and it takes 3 hours."

---

## Current Workflow

**Monday morning:** Review Slack (1 hour), scan Jira (30 min), check in on active sprint
**Mid-week:** Customer calls, design reviews, spec writing
**Thursday:** Prep for Friday stakeholder updates; review Gong calls if time allows
**Friday:** Write stakeholder updates, update Jira priorities, prep for weekly PM meeting

**Signal synthesis:** Manual, ad hoc, and incomplete. Alex reviews the most recently surfaced signals, not necessarily the most important ones.

---

## Jobs to Be Done

- **When** preparing for a prioritization decision: assemble all relevant signals from across tools so the decision is grounded in the fullest available evidence
- **When** writing a PRD: generate a first draft from the opportunity brief and customer evidence, with citations that can be verified
- **When** challenged by a stakeholder: retrieve the evidence that supports the priority decision, formatted for the audience
- **When** writing a weekly stakeholder update: personalize the same evidence for different audiences without writing three different documents from scratch

---

## How ProductPilot OS Helps

**Signal Intelligence Hub:** Alex reviews a prioritized signal feed each morning instead of switching between 6 tabs. High-severity signals are flagged automatically. Emerging patterns (3 customers mentioning the same integration issue this week) are surfaced before Alex has to notice them manually.

**Opportunity Brief Generator:** When Alex decides to investigate a potential priority, the system synthesizes all available evidence into a structured brief in 90 seconds — with a confidence score, business impact estimate, and cited evidence table. Alex reviews, edits, and approves.

**PRD Copilot:** From the approved opportunity brief, the PRD Agent generates a first draft with sections, acceptance criteria, user stories, and citations from the organizational signal corpus. Alex edits from an 80% draft instead of starting from a blank page.

**Stakeholder Alignment Center:** From the approved decision, the Stakeholder Alignment Agent generates personalized update drafts for each audience. Alex reviews and sends in 20 minutes instead of writing from scratch for 3 hours.

---

## Success Criteria

- Signal-to-brief time: <2 hours (down from 6–8 hours)
- PRD first-draft quality: PM edits <30 minutes (down from 3+ hours of drafting)
- Evidence-backing rate: >80% of priority decisions in 90 days
- Stakeholder update time: <20 minutes/week (down from 3 hours)
- Promotion to Senior PM: within 18 months, citing evidence-backed decision quality as primary differentiator
