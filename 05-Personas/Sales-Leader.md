# Persona: Sales Leader

## Overview

**Name:** Roberto Diaz
**Title:** VP of Sales
**Company:** 280-person B2B SaaS (Series C), compliance automation platform
**Team:** 10 AEs, 3 SDRs, $35M pipeline managed
**Experience:** 9 years in enterprise sales, 18 months at current company

Roberto manages a sales team that frequently encounters feature gaps as blockers in late-stage enterprise deals. His team tracks these gaps in Salesforce opportunity notes and a shared spreadsheet — neither of which creates a reliable path to the product roadmap. He needs the product team to see the revenue implications of roadmap decisions in real time, and he needs faster, more reliable feedback on when requested features will be available.

---

## Goals

- Ensure that features blocking significant ARR reach the product roadmap with full business context
- Reduce the time lost in sales cycles due to "we're building that" promises that never materialize
- Get reliable, timely feature timelines that AEs can communicate to prospects without overcommitting
- Build a trusted, systematic channel between sales signals and product decisions
- Reduce the time AEs spend on product-related escalation (writing emails, scheduling meetings with PMs)

---

## Pain Points

**Pain 1: Feature gap signals lost in the noise**
"My AEs have 10 lost deal post-mortems per quarter where 'missing feature' is in the top 3 reasons. These are documented in Salesforce. I send a monthly summary email to the product team. I don't know what happens after that. The features are still missing 6 months later in most cases."

**Pain 2: ACV context invisible in product decisions**
"When I tell the product team that 3 deals are blocked by a missing feature, they hear '3 deals.' They don't see that those 3 deals represent $1.8M in ACV, 2 of them are competitive displacement opportunities, and all 3 have a Q3 close date that requires the feature to be available in the next 8 weeks. The business context doesn't travel with the signal."

**Pain 3: Informal, untracked roadmap commitments**
"My AEs make informal roadmap commitments to prospects. 'That feature is on our roadmap' is said 20 times a quarter. These commitments are not tracked. When features don't ship on the implied timeline, customers cite 'missed commitment' as a churn reason or a trust issue. I have no visibility into which commitments are in flight."

**Pain 4: No product context for competitive displacement**
"When a prospect is evaluating us against a competitor who has a specific feature, I need to know: is that feature on our roadmap, when will it ship, and what can I say that is accurate and competitive without making a false promise? I can't get this answer quickly enough in a live sales conversation."

---

## Jobs to Be Done

- **When** logging a sales signal in Salesforce: have it automatically routed to the product team with ACV, deal timeline, and competitive context attached
- **When** a pattern of feature gaps is blocking multiple deals: receive a synthesized brief showing the aggregate ACV at risk and recommended escalation to product leadership
- **When** a prospect asks about a feature timeline: retrieve the current roadmap status and expected timeline in <2 minutes, without scheduling a PM meeting
- **When** AEs make roadmap commitments: have those commitments tracked automatically and monitored against actual delivery

---

## How ProductPilot OS Helps

**ACV-weighted signal routing:** Sales signals in Salesforce are automatically ingested, enriched with ACV and deal timeline data, and routed to the PM with a prioritization brief showing revenue at risk. The PM sees the business context without a separate email from Roberto.

**Sales gap pattern detection:** When 3+ deals are blocked by the same feature gap, the Insight Synthesizer Agent generates a sales gap brief with aggregate ACV, deal timeline urgency, and competitive context — surfaced to both Roberto and the relevant PM.

**Roadmap status queries:** Roberto and AEs can query the system: "Is multi-currency invoicing on the roadmap and when is it expected?" and receive an accurate, current-state answer from the roadmap knowledge graph.

**Commitment tracking:** When an AE notes a roadmap commitment in Salesforce or Slack ("I told them multi-currency would be available in Q3"), the system tags it and monitors the roadmap delivery against the commitment.

---

## Success Criteria

- Sales signal routing time: <5 minutes per signal (down from 30 minutes manually)
- Feature gap business impact visibility: 100% of deal-blocking gaps visible to PM with ACV context
- AE roadmap commitment tracking: 100% of CRM-logged commitments monitored
- Deal loss due to missing features: reduced by 30% within 12 months
