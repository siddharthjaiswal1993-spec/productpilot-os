# Persona: Senior Product Manager

## Overview

**Name:** Jordan Rivera
**Title:** Senior Product Manager
**Company:** 400-person B2B SaaS (Series C), HR technology platform
**Team:** 8 PMs, 3 designers, 25 engineers
**Experience:** 7 years in product, 3 years at current company

Jordan owns the enterprise tier product, which includes the highest-ACV customer segment and the most complex integration requirements. Runs the quarterly prioritization process for the enterprise product line, presents roadmap decisions to the VP Product and CTO monthly, and manages 2 IC PMs.

---

## Goals

- Maintain a defensible, evidence-backed roadmap that survives scrutiny from engineering, sales, and executive leadership
- Reduce the 4–5 hours/week spent on stakeholder updates and roadmap communications
- Scale to Staff PM by demonstrating systems thinking and strategic judgment, not just execution quality
- Build a prioritization process that the team trusts and follows, not one that gets overridden in every executive meeting

---

## Pain Points

**Pain 1: Defending priorities without assembled evidence**
"Every quarterly planning meeting, the VP asks me to defend why Enterprise SSO is prioritized over a reporting feature the CEO saw in a competitor demo. I have the answer — it's because 7 enterprise accounts covering $2.1M ARR have explicitly listed SSO as a blocking requirement. But I have to reconstruct that from memory, CRM notes, and Gong calls every single quarter. I've never had time to write it down in a way that's retrievable."

**Pain 2: The override problem**
"Even with a strong RICE score and clear prioritization rationale, a single executive request can override 3 weeks of prioritization work in one Slack message. This happens because I can't produce the counter-evidence fast enough. By the time I've assembled the data, the override has already been communicated to engineering."

**Pain 3: Stakeholder communication at scale**
"I run a monthly stakeholder update across 6 audiences: engineering, design, sales, CS, finance, and executive. Each needs different framing. I spend every last Friday of the month writing these. It's 8–10 hours of work, mostly reformatting the same information."

**Pain 4: Coaching junior PMs on evidence quality**
"My two IC PMs write PRDs that are too light on evidence. I spend 2–3 hours per PRD in review, adding customer context and citations that should have been there from the start. I don't have time to do this while also owning my own roadmap."

---

## Current Workflow

**Weekly:** Monday signals review (2 hrs), mid-week spec and engineering reviews, Thursday stakeholder prep, Friday stakeholder updates
**Monthly:** Stakeholder update document (8–10 hrs), roadmap health assessment (3 hrs), IC PM PRD reviews (4–6 hrs)
**Quarterly:** Full prioritization process (20+ hrs), quarterly business review prep (5 hrs)

---

## Jobs to Be Done

- **When** preparing for quarterly planning: assemble full evidence portfolio for all candidate priorities with business impact and confidence scores
- **When** an executive override is proposed: retrieve the evidence that supports or undermines the proposed change, formatted for a 5-minute executive discussion
- **When** reviewing a junior PM's PRD: see evidence quality score and citation coverage before reading the full document
- **When** writing monthly stakeholder updates: generate personalized drafts from shared evidence base, review in 20 minutes vs. write in 3 hours

---

## How ProductPilot OS Helps

**Prioritization Engine:** Before quarterly planning, Jordan generates scored opportunity briefs for all 20 candidate priorities. Each brief includes RICE breakdown, strategic alignment score, evidence confidence rating, and a citation table. The prioritization meeting starts from a shared evidence base, not competing intuitions.

**Executive override defense:** When an executive proposes a priority change, Jordan generates a counter-brief in 90 seconds showing the evidence for the current priority and the evidence (or lack thereof) for the proposed change. The override conversation becomes a data conversation.

**IC PM PRD coaching:** ProductPilot OS shows an evidence quality score on every PRD draft — number of cited sources, coverage across signal types, confidence score. Jordan reviews the score before the document. Low-evidence PRDs get flagged before they reach senior review.

**Stakeholder update automation:** Jordan approves the evidence base for the month and the Stakeholder Alignment Agent generates personalized drafts for all 6 audiences. Review in 30 minutes. Send. Done.

## Success Criteria

- Quarterly planning cycle time reduced from 20+ hours to <8 hours
- Executive override rate: reduced by 50% (decisions are pre-defended with evidence)
- IC PM PRD evidence quality score: >70/100 average before senior review
- Stakeholder update time: <30 minutes/month (down from 8–10 hours)
- Staff PM promotion: within 12 months
