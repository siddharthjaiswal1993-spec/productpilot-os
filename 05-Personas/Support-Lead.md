# Persona: Support Lead

## Overview

**Name:** Maya Patel
**Title:** Head of Support
**Company:** 220-person B2B SaaS (Series B), document management platform
**Team:** 12 support agents, 800 tickets/week, $18M in customer ARR managed through support interactions
**Experience:** 6 years in support, 2 years at current company

Maya manages a support team that processes 800 customer tickets per week. Her team is sitting on the richest product signal source in the company — direct customer feedback, bug reports, feature requests, and workflow frustrations — but has no systematic channel to the product roadmap. Product signals from support reach the PM team through whatever escalation mechanism each support agent uses individually: email, Slack, Jira tickets, or word of mouth.

---

## Goals

- Ensure that support signals systematically influence the product roadmap, not just the loudest or most persistent ones
- Reduce the time support agents spend on escalation communication (writing product-relevant signals into the PM team's workflow)
- Get acknowledgment and timelines on escalated product issues, so agents can close the loop with customers
- Surface volume trend patterns (rising ticket volumes in specific areas) before they become customer satisfaction crises
- Build the support function's reputation as a strategic product signal source, not just a reactive customer service function

---

## Pain Points

**Pain 1: No channel from support to product**
"We process 800 tickets a week. Maybe 50–60 of those contain genuine product signals — bugs, feature gaps, workflow limitations that are causing real customer frustration. We have no systematic way to route those signals to the product team. My agents either send an email to a PM, create a Jira ticket nobody will read, or just absorb it as customer frustration with no product action."

**Pain 2: Pattern blindness in the product team**
"We had a 40% increase in tickets about the document export function over 3 months. None of our individual escalations connected that pattern because each one was one ticket about one customer. The product team didn't see the pattern because nobody assembled the trend data. We finally escalated it as a crisis when the NPS for that feature area dropped — 3 months after the pattern was already visible in our ticket volume."

**Pain 3: Escalation overhead for support agents**
"My agents spend 30–45 minutes writing an escalation email to the product team for every significant product issue. They have to describe the issue, find the relevant tickets, summarize the customer impact, and estimate the scope — all manually. For 10 escalations per week, that's 5–7 hours of agent time on escalation administration."

**Pain 4: No feedback on escalations**
"We escalate product issues. They disappear. Three months later, a customer asks 'whatever happened to that export bug?' and we have no answer. The lack of feedback loop undermines agent morale and customer trust simultaneously."

---

## Jobs to Be Done

- **When** processing tickets: automatically flag tickets with product signal potential for routing to the PM team
- **When** ticket volume trends spike: receive a volume trend alert with the pattern, affected customers, and recommended escalation path
- **When** escalating a product issue: submit it through a structured interface that assembles context automatically rather than manually
- **When** a product decision is made on an escalated issue: receive notification with decision rationale and expected timeline

---

## How ProductPilot OS Helps

**Automatic signal classification:** Support tickets from Zendesk/Intercom are classified by the Insight Synthesizer Agent as: product bug, feature gap, workflow limitation, or customer error. Product-relevant tickets are surfaced to the PM's signal inbox automatically.

**Volume trend detection:** The Risk Watchdog Agent monitors ticket volume by category and alerts when volume trends spike beyond threshold. Maya receives a trend alert with the pattern, affected accounts, and a recommended escalation priority.

**Structured escalation interface:** When Maya's team needs to escalate a product issue, the system provides a structured form that auto-populates: ticket volume, affected accounts, ARR impact from CRM, and pattern summary. Escalation time drops from 45 minutes to 5 minutes.

**Closed-loop feedback:** When a PM makes a decision on a support-escalated signal, Maya's team receives an automatic notification with the decision context, expected timeline, and recommended customer communication template.

---

## Success Criteria

- Support signal routing: 80% of product-relevant tickets automatically classified and routed (vs. manual today)
- Volume trend detection: critical trends surfaced within 48 hours of threshold (down from 4–8 weeks to manual discovery)
- Escalation time: <10 minutes per escalation (down from 45 minutes)
- Feedback loop: 100% of escalated signals receive a decision notification within 5 business days
