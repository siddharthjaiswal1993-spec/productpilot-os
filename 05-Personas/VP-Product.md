# Persona: VP of Product

## Overview

**Name:** Sarah Kim
**Title:** VP of Product
**Company:** 500-person B2B SaaS (Series D), financial data platform
**Team:** 3 Group PMs, 12 PMs total, 2 product designers, reporting to CPO
**Experience:** 12 years in product, 18 months at current company

Sarah is responsible for the product strategy, roadmap governance, and OKR alignment for a platform with 400+ enterprise customers and $85M ARR. She reports to the CPO, presents to the board quarterly, and is accountable for the product team's decision quality, team development, and the correlation between product investment and business outcomes.

---

## Goals

- Ensure every major roadmap decision is defensible to the board, enterprise customers, and internal stakeholders with cited evidence
- Build an accountable product organization — one where decisions can be traced, questioned, and learned from
- Reduce the time she spends reviewing and correcting under-evidenced PM decisions
- Present reliable roadmap health and decision quality metrics to the CPO and board quarterly
- Develop Group PMs to own their product areas independently with high evidence quality

---

## Pain Points

**Pain 1: No visibility into team decision quality**
"I don't know how many of my team's roadmap decisions are backed by real evidence vs. assembled from memory and stakeholder pressure. I find out when decisions get challenged by engineering, sales, or the board — which is reactive and embarrassing."

**Pain 2: Roadmap defense without data infrastructure**
"When the board asks 'why are you investing in X instead of Y?' I give a strategic narrative. I don't give a cited evidence brief. I don't have one. My team has the data in their heads and their tools, but there's no system that surfaces it in a format I can use in a board context."

**Pain 3: OKR misalignment at the PM level**
"My company OKRs are clear. Whether individual PM decisions are actually aligned with those OKRs is something I can only assess in quarterly reviews. By then, 3 months of decisions have been made with potential misalignment. The feedback loop is too slow."

**Pain 4: Roadmap churn**
"We change roadmap priorities every 6–8 weeks, often because a decision was made without enough evidence and doesn't survive the first stakeholder challenge. Every priority change costs 2–3 weeks of re-planning and engineering distraction. I need decisions to be more durable."

---

## Jobs to Be Done

- **When** preparing for a board meeting: generate a portfolio-level summary of roadmap decisions with evidence quality metrics and strategic alignment scores
- **When** a PM presents a roadmap recommendation: see the evidence quality score and confidence level before the presentation
- **When** an OKR alignment review is due: generate a report showing which roadmap investments are aligned vs. misaligned with current OKRs
- **When** a stakeholder challenge comes in: retrieve the evidence basis for the challenged decision within minutes

---

## How ProductPilot OS Helps

**Portfolio intelligence dashboard:** Sarah sees evidence-backing rates, confidence scores, and OKR alignment scores across all 12 PMs on one executive dashboard. Real-time visibility into decision quality across the organization.

**Board-ready reporting:** Executive Summary Agent generates a portfolio-level intelligence brief with decision quality metrics, strategic alignment analysis, and roadmap health score — ready for board presentation with citations.

**OKR alignment monitoring:** Roadmap Intelligence Agent continuously analyzes whether active roadmap items are aligned with current OKRs and surfaces misalignments before quarterly review.

**Stakeholder defense:** When a decision is challenged, Sarah can retrieve the full evidence brief in 30 seconds — customer signals, business impact, confidence score, PM approval record — formatted for an executive audience.

## Success Criteria

- Team evidence-backing rate: >80% within 6 months
- Board presentation preparation time: <2 hours (down from 8–12 hours)
- Roadmap churn rate: reduced by 35% within 12 months
- PM decisions challenged and reversed without evidence: <10% (down from ~40%)
