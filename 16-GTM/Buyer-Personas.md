# Buyer Personas

## GTM Buyer Persona 1: The PM Champion

**Name:** Alex  
**Title:** Senior PM or Lead PM  
**Company:** Series B–C B2B SaaS, 100–500 employees  
**Team:** 3–8 PMs

**Profile:**
Alex is technically strong, outcome-obsessed, and has strong opinions about how product decisions should be made. Alex believes in evidence and is frustrated by "loudest voice" prioritization culture. Alex reads Lenny's Newsletter, follows product leaders on LinkedIn, and is the person who usually introduces new tools to the team.

**Alex's current pain:**
- Spends 3–5 hours/week gathering evidence for priority decisions that could be made much faster
- Makes strong arguments in stakeholder meetings but loses to whoever has the VP relationship
- Writes PRDs that feel thin on customer evidence because research is so time-consuming
- Wants to model good evidence-based practice for junior PMs but doesn't have time to demonstrate it consistently

**How Alex finds ProductPilot:**
Product Hunt, Lenny's Newsletter guest post, or a colleague recommendation ("you should try this").

**Alex's buying journey:**
1. Signs up for free trial (self-serve)
2. Connects Slack and Zendesk in 10 minutes
3. Generates first brief on a cluster of support tickets
4. Sends the brief to VP Product: "Look what I just made"
5. VP Product asks "how did you do this?" → expansion begins

**Alex's success moment:**
"I walked into the Tuesday planning meeting with a brief that had 8 sources, a confidence score, and a business impact estimate. My VP said 'this is exactly what I've been asking for.' That was the moment I knew this was going to stick."

## GTM Buyer Persona 2: The VP Product Champion

**Name:** Jordan  
**Title:** VP Product or Head of Product  
**Company:** Series B–C B2B SaaS, 200–800 employees  
**Team:** 5–15 PMs

**Profile:**
Jordan manages a team of PMs with varying experience levels. Jordan is responsible for product strategy and must defend roadmap decisions to the CEO, board, and enterprise customers. Jordan is increasingly aware that their team spends too much time on synthesis and not enough on actual product strategy. Jordan has tried Productboard, has an opinion on AI tools, and is skeptical of hype but open to genuine ROI.

**Jordan's current pain:**
- When asked "why is this P1?", the answer varies wildly by PM — some have evidence, some have opinions
- Spends 8–10 hours/month on executive roadmap prep that feels manually assembled
- Can't easily see which of their PMs are making evidence-backed decisions and which aren't
- Lost institutional knowledge when two strong PMs left last year — the "why" behind decisions went with them
- Engineering complains about PRDs that lack clear requirements or customer context

**How Jordan finds ProductPilot:**
Alex (PM Champion) shows Jordan the brief they generated. Or Jordan sees a VP Product LinkedIn post from a peer. Or Jordan receives a direct outreach from the ProductPilot sales team.

**Jordan's buying journey:**
1. Sees Alex's brief or receives founder/sales outreach
2. Requests a demo (or watches the async demo video)
3. Asks: "Can I see this work with a real product area from my company?"
4. Brings in 2–3 PMs for a pilot
5. Sees PM Acceptance Rate and Evidence-Backing Rate metrics after 30 days
6. Approves team-wide upgrade to Team tier

**Jordan's success moment:**
"At the last QPR, every priority decision my team presented had an evidence brief attached. The board asked one tough question about our enterprise bet. One of my PMs pulled up the brief, clicked on the Gong citation, and played the 30-second call excerpt. The room went quiet. That's the standard I want now."

**Jordan's approval authority:**
Team tier ($99/seat/month): Jordan approves without CFO involvement at most Series B–C companies. Annual commitment of $10K–$50K is within Jordan's budget authority.

## GTM Buyer Persona 3: The Enterprise Buyer

**Name:** David  
**Title:** CPO (Chief Product Officer)  
**Company:** Series C–D enterprise SaaS, 500–2,000 employees  
**Team:** 20–60 PMs across multiple product lines

**Profile:**
David oversees multiple product areas and is accountable to the board for product outcomes. David thinks at the portfolio level: which bets are evidence-backed, which are assumptions, and where is the organization flying blind. David needs tools that scale across the organization, meet enterprise security requirements, and can be defended in board conversations.

**David's current pain:**
- Cannot see how decisions are being made across 20+ PMs — some have evidence practices, most don't
- Loses strategic context when senior PMs leave; the institutional memory walks out the door
- Board wants evidence that product decisions are evidence-based, not just asserted
- Engineering leaders complain that PRDs from different PMs have wildly different quality standards
- Sales reports that roadmap commitments are being made by individual PMs without central visibility

**David's buying journey:**
1. Jordan (VP Product) presents the tool with a 90-day results summary
2. David reviews the Decision Quality Report and Evidence-Backing Rate trend
3. IT/Security reviews the security documentation (SOC 2 readiness, DPA)
4. Legal reviews the contract and data processing terms
5. David approves enterprise contract; company-wide rollout begins

**David's approval requirement:**
SOC 2, DPA, MSA, security review — all required before David signs. Enterprise pricing ($100K+/year) requires CEO awareness and budget allocation.
