# Competitive Battlecards

## How to Use These

These battlecards are for conversations where prospects bring up specific competitor tools. The goal is not to disparage competitors — it's to clarify why ProductPilot OS does something categorically different.

---

## Battlecard 1: vs. Productboard

**When a prospect says:** "We already use Productboard for prioritization."

**Acknowledge:** "Productboard is excellent for organizing ideas and features. It's a great feedback inbox and roadmap tool."

**Differentiate:**

| Dimension | Productboard | ProductPilot OS |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Core job | Organize and prioritize feature ideas | Synthesize signals and generate evidence-backed decisions |
| AI capability | AI-assisted tagging and organization | 8 specialized agents for synthesis, prioritization, PRD, risk, stakeholder alignment |
| Evidence | PM manually adds evidence to features | Automatically retrieves and cites evidence from connected sources |
| Citations | Not a native feature | Every claim cited to specific source, author, date, excerpt |
| Confidence | Not measured | 0–100 score with 5-component breakdown |
| Knowledge Graph | Feature-centric database | Entity-relationship model: features, customers, signals, decisions, outcomes |
| PRD support | Via templates (basic) | AI-generated, evidence-cited PRD from approved brief |

**The key point:** "Productboard manages the backlog. ProductPilot answers 'why should this be in the backlog?' and 'why should it be at the top?'"

**Where they coexist:** Many teams use both. ProductPilot generates the evidence-backed brief and priority recommendation; the PM then updates the Productboard backlog with that decision.

## Battlecard 2: vs. Notion AI

**When a prospect says:** "We use Notion for everything, and Notion AI has AI writing features."

**Acknowledge:** "Notion is a great workspace tool, and Notion AI is solid for writing assistance."

**Differentiate:**

| Dimension | Notion AI | ProductPilot OS |
|-----------|----------|----------------|
| Problem solved | Writing assistance and search within Notion | Signal synthesis from 14+ connected sources |
| Data sources | Notion documents only | Slack, Jira, Zendesk, Gong, Salesforce, Notion, Confluence, and more |
| Agent system | None | 8 specialized PM workflow agents |
| Evidence retrieval | Based on Notion content | RAG across all connected sources with confidence scoring |
| PM-specific features | None | Opportunity briefs, RICE scoring, PRD copilot, Risk Watchdog, Stakeholder Alignment |
| Knowledge Graph | No | Yes — entities, relationships, decisions, outcomes |

**The key point:** "Notion AI helps you write faster inside Notion. ProductPilot synthesizes signals from across your entire tech stack — from Slack discussions to Gong calls to Zendesk tickets — and turns them into PM-ready decisions."

## Battlecard 4: vs. ChatGPT / Claude (Generic AI)

**When a prospect says:** "Couldn't our PMs just use ChatGPT for this?"

**Acknowledge:** "General AI assistants are genuinely useful for a lot of writing and thinking tasks."

**Differentiate:**

| Dimension | ChatGPT / Claude | ProductPilot OS |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Data source | Model training data + manual paste | Connected to your actual organizational data |
| Evidence | Fabricates plausible evidence | Cites specific sources from your corpus |
| Hallucination | No systematic prevention | Citation binding + post-generation validation |
| PM workflow | Generic chat interface | 8 specialized PM workflow agents |
| Knowledge accumulation | No memory across sessions | Product Knowledge Graph compounds over time |
| HITL design | Not present | Every consequential action requires PM approval |
| Audit trail | None | Immutable decision audit log |

**The key point:** "You can ask ChatGPT to write a PRD. It will write a convincing one. But it will fabricate customer needs, invent feature requests, and cite evidence that doesn't exist. ProductPilot cites your actual customers, your actual Zendesk tickets, your actual Gong calls."

## Battlecard 5: vs. Gong

**When a prospect says:** "We use Gong for voice of customer."

**Acknowledge:** "Gong is the best in class for revenue intelligence from sales calls. It's a powerful tool."

**Differentiate:**

| Dimension | Gong | ProductPilot OS |
|-----------|------|----------------|
| Core job | Revenue intelligence from sales calls | Product intelligence from all signals |
| Signal sources | Sales call transcripts | 14+ sources including Gong transcripts |
| Product workflow | Not a product management tool | Purpose-built for PM workflows |
| Brief generation | Not a feature | Evidence-backed opportunity briefs in 90 seconds |
| Decision support | Not a feature | RICE scoring, PRD, risk alerts, stakeholder alignment |

**Where they coexist:** Gong is a source. ProductPilot ingests Gong transcripts alongside Zendesk tickets, Slack messages, and CRM notes — and synthesizes them into PM-ready intelligence.

**The key point:** "Gong tells you what customers said in sales calls. ProductPilot synthesizes what customers are saying everywhere — including Gong — and turns it into product decisions."
