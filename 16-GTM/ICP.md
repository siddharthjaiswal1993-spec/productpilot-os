# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

## Primary ICP: Product-Led B2B SaaS (Series A–C)

### Firmographic Criteria

| Dimension | Target Range | Why |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Company stage | Series A–C | Enough PM headcount to need coordination; not too large that procurement takes 12 months |
| PM team size | 3–15 PMs | Small enough for PMs to influence tooling; large enough to feel the coordination problem |
| Company size | 50–600 employees | Product team has autonomy; signal volume is high enough to overwhelm manual synthesis |
| Revenue | $3M–$50M ARR | Budget exists for PM tooling; feels the pain of inefficient product decisions |
| Business model | B2B SaaS / product-led | Product decisions directly tied to revenue; signal sources (Zendesk, Jira, Gong) are standard |
| Growth rate | >25% YoY | Fast-growing teams feel the coordination problem acutely; decision speed matters |

### Tech Stack Signals (Ideal Fit)

The presence of these tools in the tech stack signals both the right problem space and integration compatibility:

| Category | Tools | Signal |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Project tracking | Jira or Linear | Engineering/PM workflow is structured; backlog prioritization is a real process |
| Communication | Slack | Signal volume is high; information is distributed across channels |
| Customer support | Zendesk or Intercom | Customer feedback exists but is fragmented |
| CRM | Salesforce or HubSpot | Deal data is structured; revenue context is available for prioritization |
| Conversation intel | Gong or Chorus | Voice of customer from sales calls is being recorded (not indexed) |

**Strong-fit signal:** Company uses 3+ of the above tools and has a dedicated VP Product or Head of Product.

## Secondary ICP: Mid-Market Enterprise SaaS (Series C–D)

### Firmographic Criteria

| Dimension | Target Range |
|-----------|-------------|
| Company stage | Series C–D |
| PM team size | 10–50 PMs |
| Company size | 500–2,000 employees |
| Revenue | $25M–$150M ARR |

**Why this is secondary (not primary):**
- Longer sales cycles (3–6 months vs. 2–6 weeks for primary ICP)
- Security/compliance requirements create procurement friction
- Require SSO, SOC 2, advanced permissions model (V2 features)
- Higher ACV potential ($150K+ annually) justifies enterprise sales motion

**Entry strategy:** Land at the product team level (IC PM champion) → expand to VP Product → formalize as enterprise deal.

---

## Anti-ICP: Who We Do Not Target

| Profile | Why Not |
|---------|---------|
| Pre-product/idea stage startups | No signal corpus; no PM team to adopt; no budget |
| Consumer apps (B2C) | Different signal sources; customer intelligence layer doesn't translate to B2C |
| Non-technical teams (marketing agencies) | No Jira/Gong/Zendesk; no product decisions to support |
| Large enterprises (pre-SOC 2, pre-SSO) | Cannot close deals without enterprise security features (V2) |
| PM solo (single PM) | Not enough signal volume; no coordination problem |

## ICP Qualification Scorecard

Sales and CS use this scorecard to qualify inbound leads:

| Criterion | 0 points | 1 point | 2 points |
|-----------|----------|---------|----------|
| PM team size | <2 PMs | 2–5 PMs | 6+ PMs |
| Uses Jira or Linear | No | — | Yes |
| Uses Slack | No | — | Yes |
| Uses Zendesk or Intercom | No | — | Yes |
| Uses Salesforce or HubSpot | No | — | Yes |
| VP Product or Head of Product exists | No | — | Yes |
| Series A or later | Pre-Series A | Series A | Series B+ |

**Score 10–12:** Strong fit — prioritize in pipeline  
**Score 7–9:** Good fit — proceed with qualification  
**Score 5–6:** Marginal fit — assess before investing sales time  
**Score <5:** Poor fit — deprioritize

## Buyer vs. User vs. Champion

| Role | Relationship to ProductPilot | Key Concern |
|------|------------------------------|------------|
| **IC PM** (User) | Daily user; primary value receiver | Will this save me time? Can I trust it? |
| **VP Product / Head of Product** (Champion) | Drives team adoption; authorizes spending | Will this make my team better? Does this give me visibility? |
| **CPO** (Economic buyer at Enterprise) | Approves budget; needs ROI narrative | What is the business case? How does this reduce decision risk? |
| **IT / Security** (Gatekeeper at Enterprise) | Approves integration; reviews security | Is my data safe? Does this meet our compliance requirements? |

**PLG motion (Primary ICP):** IC PM self-serves → shows VP Product → VP Product upgrades team

**Top-down motion (Enterprise):** VP Product sees ROI narrative → involves CPO → involves IT → deal closes
