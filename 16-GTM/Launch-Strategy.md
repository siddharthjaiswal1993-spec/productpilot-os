# Launch Strategy

## Launch Philosophy

ProductPilot OS is a product that must be experienced to be believed. The core value — watching a 3-hour synthesis task happen in 90 seconds with citations you can verify — is not something you can communicate through a landing page.

The launch strategy is designed around proof: getting the right PMs to experience the product, giving them language to describe what they experienced, and letting that language drive the next wave of adoption.

---

## Pre-Launch: Design Partner Program (Months 1–3)

**Goal:** 3–5 paying design partners who will become public case studies and reference customers.

**Target design partners:**
- 2–3 B2B SaaS companies at Series B, 5–10 PM team size
- At least 1 with a well-known CPO or VP Product who will speak publicly
- At least 1 in a visible vertical (FinTech, MarTech, or DevTools)
- At least 1 with a strong technical PM team (Staff PM or Principal PM level)

**Design partner commitments:**
- Monthly 90-minute feedback sessions
- Share usage metrics (with permission)
- Participate in monthly human evaluation sessions
- Provide a case study (written or video) within 60 days of launch milestone
- Reference availability for sales conversations

**Design partner pricing:** 50% discount on Team tier in exchange for commitments above.

**Design partner outreach:**
1. Founder network and warm introductions (first 10 outreach attempts)
2. PM community (Lenny's Newsletter, Mind the Product, Product-Led Alliance) posts
3. LinkedIn founder content: "We're looking for 3 product teams to help shape a new way of working"

## V1 Launch: Product Hunt + PM Community (Month 4–5)

**Goal:** 100 qualified signups; 20 activated (generated at least 3 briefs); 5 paid conversions in first 30 days.

### Product Hunt Launch

**Preparation (Month 3–4):**
- Build hunter relationships; identify hunter with PM community credibility
- Create demo video (2–3 minutes): full brief generation flow with real data
- Design landing page to convert Product Hunt traffic
- Build review template for design partners to submit day-of-launch

**Day-of-launch assets:**
- Product Hunt tagline: "The intelligence layer for product decisions — AI that cites its sources"
- Hero demo: 60-second screen recording showing signal → brief → citations
- Design partner quote panel (3 quotes from beta users)
- Launch thread on X/Twitter by founder with demo screenshots

**Follow-up (Days 2–7):**
- Respond to every Product Hunt comment
- DM top upvoters about feedback
- Post "what we learned from PH launch" thread (builds credibility + extends reach)

### PM Community Launch

**Lenny's Newsletter / Substack:**
- Guest post: "Why 80% of product decisions are based on one person's loudest opinion"
- CTA: "We built a tool to fix this. 50 PMs in closed beta — apply here."

**Product-Led Community (Product School, Mind the Product):**
- Workshop: "Evidence-backed prioritization — how to make decisions your VP Product can't override"
- Workshop attendees get early access

**LinkedIn thought leadership (founder/team):**
- Series: "Signal Collapse — why PM teams drown in feedback but still make gut decisions"
- Post 1: The problem (high engagement, no CTA)
- Post 2: The framework (evidence-backed prioritization)
- Post 3: What we built
- Post 4: Design partner story (with permission)

## Growth Loop: PLG Motion

The primary growth loop is product-led:

```
PM discovers ProductPilot (Product Hunt, LinkedIn, community, colleague)
    │
    ▼
PM signs up (Starter, 2-seat minimum)
    │
    ▼
PM generates first brief within 7 days (activation event)
    │
    ▼
PM shows brief to VP Product → "How did you make this so quickly?"
    │
    ▼
VP Product signs up; invites team → Expansion event
    │
    ▼
Team reaches Starter limit → Upgrade to Team
    │
    ▼
VP Product shows Executive Summary to CPO → Enterprise conversation
```

**Key activation metric:** "7-day brief generation" — did the PM generate at least 3 briefs in the first 7 days? This predicts 30-day retention at >80%.

**Key expansion metric:** "Invite rate" — how many Starter PMs invite at least 1 teammate within 14 days? Target: >40%.
