# Sales Motion

## Sales Philosophy

ProductPilot OS is a product-led growth company. The primary sales motion is: PM discovers product → PM experiences value → PM invites team → team invites VP → VP becomes champion. Sales assists but does not replace this motion.

Enterprise deals require a sales-assisted motion from the start. The ICP and deal size determine which motion applies.

---

## Sales Motion by ICP Segment

### Segment 1: PLG Self-Serve (Starter, up to $12K ACV)
**Motion:** Pure product-led  
**No SDR or AE involvement**

Flow:
1. PM signs up via website
2. Onboarding email sequence (Day 0, Day 3, Day 7)
3. Activation: first brief generated within 7 days
4. Expansion: PM invites colleagues; reaches seat limit → upgrades

**Success metric:** 7-day activation rate >40%; 30-day retention >70%

### Segment 2: Sales-Assisted (Team, $10K–$50K ACV)
**Motion:** Inbound-led with sales assist  
**AE involvement at Team tier**

Flow:
1. PM discovers product (PLG, Product Hunt, community)
2. PM starts Starter trial
3. PM generates 5+ briefs → activation signal triggers sales outreach
4. AE books discovery call with PM champion
5. Demo: customized to the PM's actual product area (if possible) or using sample data
6. Trial-to-paid: PM gets 14-day full-feature trial before annual commitment
7. Close: VP Product approval typically required

**Discovery call structure (30 min):**
- (5 min) Listen: "What's your current process for going from a customer signal to a priority decision?"
- (5 min) Diagnose: "How much time does your team spend each week synthesizing signals and writing briefs?"
- (15 min) Demo: show brief generation → evidence table → prioritization → stakeholder alignment
- (5 min) CTA: "Would you want your team trying this for 2 weeks?"

### Segment 3: Enterprise (Custom, $36K–$200K+ ACV)
**Motion:** Top-down sales with champion development  
**Named account AE + SE involvement**

Entry points:
- PLG champion becomes enterprise buyer
- Outbound to VP Product at target account (warm intro preferred)
- Referral from existing enterprise customer

Enterprise sales cycle: 3–6 months  
Key stakeholders: VP Product (champion), CPO (economic buyer), IT/Security (gatekeeper), Legal (procurement)

**Enterprise deal stages:**
| Stage | Milestone | Goal |
|-------|-----------|------|
| Discovery | VP Product demo completed | Understand value; identify pain |
| Technical validation | Security review completed; IT approved | Remove gatekeeper blockers |
| Champion development | VP Product is actively selling internally | Champion presents to CPO |
| Business case | ROI model approved by CPO | Formal proposal requested |
| Procurement | Legal/security final approval | Contract terms agreed |
| Close | Contract signed | Kickoff scheduled |

## Demo Strategy

**Demo structure (30 minutes):**

**Segment 1 (5 min): "The Problem You Recognize"**
Show the PM's current reality: 12 signal sources, fragmented context, the "loudest voice wins" meeting. Use language from the PM's own world ("How many tabs do you have open on a Monday morning?").

**Segment 2 (15 min): "The Moment That Changes Everything"**
Live demo of the full brief generation flow:
1. Signal Hub → point to a cluster (6 signals about the same topic)
2. Click "Generate Brief" → 60-second wait → brief appears
3. Click a citation → citation panel opens → show the actual Gong transcript excerpt
4. Expand confidence score → show the 5-component breakdown
5. Click "Approve Brief" → downstream actions unlock
6. Generate prioritization brief → show RICE with evidence per dimension
7. Generate 3 stakeholder drafts → show Engineering vs. Sales framing difference

**Segment 3 (10 min): "Your World, Not Our Demo"**
Address the specific pain they mentioned in discovery. "You said you spend 3 hours on Fridays writing stakeholder updates — this is what that looks like with ProductPilot." Ask: "If you could generate a prioritization brief like this for your top 5 backlog items in the next 30 minutes, what would that change for you?"

## Sales Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|---------|
| "We're worried about AI making stuff up" | "That's exactly why we built citations-first. Every claim links to a specific source. You don't trust the AI — you verify the source. The AI is the researcher; you're still the author." |
| "We use Productboard / Jira AI" | "Great — ProductPilot complements both. We're the intelligence layer above the tools, not a replacement. Many teams use ProductPilot to generate the evidence-backed case, then update their Productboard backlog." |
| "Our data is sensitive — I'm worried about security" | "We understand. We're SOC 2 Type I compliant today and SOC 2 Type II in progress. We'd be happy to share our security documentation." |
| "We don't have enough signals for this to work" | "If you're using Slack, Jira, and Zendesk, you have more signals than you can process. Let's look at a 30-day signal snapshot from your tools." |
| "The PM team is too small" | "Our minimum is 2 seats. A 2-PM team can start at $98/month — less than most software subscriptions your team uses." |

## Customer Success Motion

**Onboarding goal:** First brief generated within 7 days; first approved decision within 30 days.

**30-day onboarding playbook:**
1. Day 0: Welcome + integration setup guide + "your first brief in 15 minutes"
2. Day 3: Check-in email: "Have you connected your Slack and Zendesk?"
3. Day 7: Activation check: if no briefs generated, customer success call
4. Day 14: "How's it going?" call — identify friction; offer to co-generate first brief
5. Day 30: Success check: "What decisions have you made differently?"

**Expansion trigger:** Team generates 10+ approved briefs → CS initiates upgrade conversation.
