# Category Creation Strategy

## The Category We're Creating

**Category name:** Product Intelligence OS

This is not a roadmap tool. Not a project management add-on. Not a chatbot for PMs. It is a new category: the intelligence layer that sits above all PM tools and synthesizes signals into evidence-backed product decisions.

---

## Why Category Creation vs. Category Entry

We could position ProductPilot OS as "a better Productboard" or "AI for Jira." We are not doing this. Here's why:

**Category entry (competing in an existing category):**
- Competes for existing budget and attention
- Differentiated by features
- Evaluated by "how is this better than what I have?"
- Acquisitions: easy for incumbents to neutralize with a feature release or acquisition

**Category creation (defining a new category):**
- Creates new budget and justification
- Differentiated by category definition
- Evaluated by "does this category problem exist and is this the best solution?"
- Acquisitions: validates the category rather than neutralizes the entrant

The case for category creation: the problem ProductPilot solves (Signal Collapse, decision quality, institutional memory) does not map cleanly to any existing product category. Forcing it into an existing category would underrepresent the scope of what we're building and what the market needs.

## The Category Creation Playbook

### Step 1: Name the Problem
Before we can sell a solution, the market needs to recognize the problem. "Signal Collapse" is our name for the core problem — PM teams drowning in fragmented signals with no synthesis system.

**Evidence the problem is real:**
- Every PM we've spoken to has described this problem without prompting
- The synthesis work (3–5 hours/week) is consistently identified as the biggest workflow drain
- VP Products consistently describe "decision quality" as a gap with no current solution

**Naming goal:** When a PM says "we have a Signal Collapse problem" in a meeting, the category is real.

### Step 2: Name the Category
"Product Intelligence OS" is a deliberate category name. It:
- Implies operating system status (foundational infrastructure, not an app)
- Implies intelligence (not just data collection or workflow management)
- Is specific to product management (not a generic "AI decision platform")
- Can be defended against dilution (unlike "AI PM tool")

**Category vocabulary we own:**
- Signal Collapse
- Evidence-Backing Rate
- PM Acceptance Rate
- Decision Debt
- Product Intelligence OS

### Step 3: Establish the Vocabulary
Category creation requires vocabulary that the market adopts. We need PMs to use "evidence-backing rate" in roadmap reviews, "decision debt" in engineering conversations, and "signal collapse" in VP Product discussions.

**Vocabulary dissemination channels:**
- Guest posts in Lenny's Newsletter, Mind the Product, Product School
- Conference talks: "Evidence-backed prioritization" framework
- Free resources: "Product Intelligence OS" framework whitepaper
- Founder LinkedIn content: consistent use of category vocabulary
- Customer case studies: vocabulary woven into customer descriptions of their problem

### Step 4: Lighthouse Customers
Category creation requires lighthouse customers: well-known companies in the ICP who publicly adopt and advocate for the category.

**Target lighthouse customers (Year 1):**
- A well-known Series B/C SaaS company with a visible VP Product or CPO
- A company in a high-profile vertical (FinTech, MarTech, DevTools)
- A company whose CPO or VP Product has an active LinkedIn/conference presence

**Lighthouse customer requirements:**
- Public case study (blog post or video)
- Named reference for analyst and press conversations
- Speaker availability for conference panels
- Willing to use "Product Intelligence OS" vocabulary in public

### Step 5: Analyst and Press Coverage
By Month 12, the goal is that a Gartner or Forrester analyst has mentioned "product intelligence" as an emerging category. Not a full MQ — just a mention in a research note.

**Analyst strategy:**
- Brief Gartner, Forrester, and IDC analysts on the category (Month 9–12)
- Provide research framework: define the category, size the market, identify the problem
- Connect analysts with lighthouse customer references

**Press strategy:**
- TechCrunch, VentureBeat: product launch story with lighthouse customer quotes
- PM media (Lenny's Newsletter, Product-Led Alliance): category creation narrative
- General AI press: "AI tools transforming [industry]" narratives

## The Risk: Category Too Narrow

The primary risk of category creation is naming a category that's too narrow to support a large company. "Product Intelligence OS" is intentionally broad:
- It applies to any size PM team (3 PMs to 300 PMs)
- It applies to any B2B product company (not just SaaS)
- It grows with the product (V1 = individual intelligence; V2 = team intelligence; V3 = org intelligence)
- It is expansible into adjacent areas (engineering intelligence, design intelligence, business intelligence)

The goal is a category wide enough to support a $500M+ ARR company while narrow enough to be clearly distinct from existing categories.
