# SUMMARY: ProductPilot OS Documentation Repository

This document is the meta-guide to the ProductPilot OS documentation repository. It explains what was built, what matters most, how to navigate it, and how to use it as a portfolio artifact.

---

## What Was Created

This repository contains the complete product, strategy, architecture, and go-to-market blueprint for ProductPilot OS — an AI-native product intelligence and decision operating system. The documentation spans 19 sections and 100+ files.

### Section-by-Section Breakdown

**01-Vision** — Five documents establishing the foundational purpose of ProductPilot OS: the long-horizon vision for product intelligence infrastructure, the operational mission, the core product thesis (signal collapse as the central problem), the why-now timing argument, and the category narrative for "Product Intelligence OS."

**02-Market-Research** — Five documents establishing market context: a detailed competitive landscape analysis across five competitive categories, market trend analysis, category creation playbook, and comparable company analysis.

**03-Problem-Space** — Six documents unpacking the problem in depth: signal collapse (the core), the prioritization crisis, the PM context-switching tax (quantified), the stakeholder alignment infrastructure gap, product decision debt, and the evidence gap.

**04-Product-Strategy** — Eight documents establishing the full strategic position: product strategy, north star metric, product principles, strategic bets, success metrics framework, positioning, differentiation analysis, and business model.

**05-Personas** — Ten detailed persona documents covering every user type: Product Manager, Senior PM, Staff PM, Group PM, VP Product, CPO, Engineering Manager, Customer Success Lead, Sales Leader, and Support Lead.

**06-User-Journeys** — Seven detailed end-to-end user journey maps covering the core workflows with before/after time and quality comparisons.

**07-Opportunity-Briefs** — Five documents providing reusable templates and a fully worked example (Enterprise SSO blocking $2.1M in expansion).

**08-Product-Requirements** — Seven documents covering the full requirements layer: PRD, MVP scope, functional requirements (50+), non-functional requirements, acceptance criteria, edge cases (30+), and metrics instrumentation (50+ events).

**09-Agent-System** — Ten documents specifying every AI agent including inputs, outputs, reasoning patterns, autonomy levels, guardrails, and failure modes.

**10-AI-Architecture** — Eight documents covering the full AI architecture: strategy, agentic architecture, reasoning engine, decision engine, confidence engine, trust layer, human-in-the-loop, and system diagrams.

**11-RAG-Architecture** — Ten documents specifying the retrieval-augmented generation system: overview through freshness management.

**12-Evaluation-Framework** — Nine documents covering the eval stack through PM acceptance rate measurement.

**13-Risk-Governance** — Eight documents covering responsible AI, PII, security, compliance, approvals, audit logs, permissions, and failure recovery.

**14-Roadmap** — Four documents: V1 MVP through Long-Term Roadmap (3-7 years).

**15-Prototype** — Seven documents: UX principles, page structure, screen specs, Lovable prompt, and Bolt prompt.

**16-GTM** — Eight documents: ICP, positioning, messaging, pricing, launch strategy, sales motion, buyer personas, and battlecards.

**17-Investor-Narrative** — Six documents: why-now through full investor memo.

**18-Technical-Architecture** — Ten documents: system architecture, integrations (Slack, Jira, CRM, Support, Analytics), data pipeline, sequence diagrams, and API design.

**19-Future-Vision** — Five documents covering the long-horizon future of product management and the Product OS vision.
